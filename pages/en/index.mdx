## What Is a Good Reaction Time?

Reaction time is the time between a stimulus (e.g. screen change) and your response (e.g. click). For adults, **200–250 ms** is typical; **150–200 ms** is very good; pros often sit in **100–150 ms**. Our **reaction time test** gives you a score in ms so you can see where you stand.

| Reaction time (ms) | Level        |
|--------------------|-------------|
| 150–200            | Very fast   |
| 201–250            | Good        |
| 251–300            | Average     |
| 301–400            | Slower      |
| Over 400            | Worth retesting or checking habits (sleep, focus, device) |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is reaction time?

Reaction time is the delay between a stimulus (e.g. something you see or hear) and your response (e.g. click or key press). A **reaction time test** measures this in milliseconds.

### What is the average human reaction time?

For visual stimuli, the average is about **200–250 ms**. It can vary with age, fitness, sleep, and practice.

### Is 200 ms reaction time good?

Yes. **200 ms** is above average. Many people are around 250 ms, so 200 ms is considered good.

### Can you improve reaction time?

Yes. Regular practice (e.g. taking a **reaction time test**), good sleep, hydration, and exercise can help improve and maintain faster reflexes.
